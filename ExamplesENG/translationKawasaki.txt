MALE, 2 AA and 8 MONTHS
Medical history:
- Pathological proximate: The patient is brought to the PS by the parents for persistent high fever for 6 days (TC max 40.1°C), unresponsive to home antipyretic therapy (paracetamol and ibuprofen at doses appropriate to weight). The parents report the appearance over the last 2-3 days of reddening of the eyes without discharge, a rash on the trunk, chapped and reddened lips, and an unusual-looking tongue. They also report right laterocervical swelling. The child is irritable, refuses to play and feed. Denies mother episodes of coughing, rhinorrhoea or diarrhoea. No known contact with ill subjects.
Remote pathology: Born at term, eutococcal birth, regular psychomotor development. Vaccinations in order for age. No known pathology. No previous access to the PS. No continuous home therapy. Denies mother known drug allergies.

Objective examination: Patient alert, irritable, crying at assessment. General condition compromised. Vesicular murmur present in all lung areas, no added noise. Tachycardic, rhythmic heart tones, free pauses. No appreciable murmurs. Abdomen treatable, mild pain on diffuse palpation, no organomegaly evident.

Clinical diary:
- Found venous access and performed biochemistry, blood count, indices of inflammation, procalcitonin, ESR, fibrinogen, ALT/AST, bilirubin, albumin, sodium, potassium, renal function, urinalysis. Blood cultures x2 are performed before any antibiotic therapy, to exclude bacteremia.
- ECG is performed: sinus tachycardia, HR 138 bpm, no ST segment abnormalities, no conduction disturbances, PR interval normal for age.
- Tests are viewed: neutrophilic leukocytosis (GB 18,600/μL, N 78%). PCR 14.2 mg/dL. ESR 88 mm/h. Fibrinogen 620 mg/dL (high). Procalcitonin 2.4 ng/mL. Platelets
498,000/μL (thrombocytosis). ALT slightly elevated. Albumin 2.8 g/dL (hypoalbuminemia). Urine examination: sterile leukocyturia. Sodium 132 mEq/L (mild hyponatriemia).
- The paediatrician on call is urgently contacted for collegial assessment. The clinical picture persistent fever for more than 5 days associated with all 5 clinical criteria for Kawasaki disease is diagnostic for Kawasaki disease in its classical form.
- Clinical criteria noted: - Fever for 6 days (TC 39.8°C in PS) - Bilateral non-exudative conjunctivitis - Alterations of the lips and oral cavity: erythematous and chapped lips, strawberry tongue - Polymorphous rash on trunk and root of limbs - Right cervical lymphadenopathy with a single lymph node about 2cm wide, painful, not fluctuating - Erythema and mild oedema of the hands and feet PA 96/58 mmHg. HR 138 bpm. SpO2 99% aa. TC 39.8°C. Weight 14kg.
- Bedside echo performed by paediatrician: cardiac function visually preserved, coronary arteries not clearly dilated by this method; dedicated inpatient echocardiography reserved.
- Paracetamol 15mg/kg e.v. (210mg) is administered for fever management pending admission.
- The family is informed of the Diagnosis, the need for urgent hospitalisation and the importance of early treatment to reduce the risk of coronary aneurysms.

Diagnosis: KAWASAKI DISEASE - CLASSIC FORM (5/5 criteria present)
Prognosis: urgent hospitalisation
