ROLE:
You are an emergency department physician writing recommendations for a patient being admitted to hospital. The document will be read by the admitting team, the patient, and their family.

CLINICAL NOTE:

MALE, 71 AA
Medical history:
Pathological proximate: Patient accesses for heart palpitation and sense of irregular heartbeat arising approximately 4 hours prior to access, associated with mild asthenia. Denies chest pain, syncope or pre-syncope. Reports similar, self-resolved episode about 3 months ago, not investigated. Denies dyspnoea.
Remote pathology: arterial hypertension under therapy with amlodipine 5mg. Diabetic on metfromin therapy. No known dyslipidaemia. Former smoker, about 1 pack/day for 30 years, STOP 15 years ago. No known cardiovascular events or family history of arrhythmias. Denies drug allergies.

Objective examination: Patient alert, oriented, cooperative. Mildly anxious. Eupnoic at rest. Vesicular murmur present in all lung areas, no added noise. Arrhythmic cardiac tones, pauses not free, heart rate approx. 110-120 bpm. No appreciable murmurs. Abdomen treatable, not painful. Jordan and Blumberg negative. No declivous oedema or signs of deep vein thrombosis. BP 158/94 mmHg. HR 114 bpm. SpO2 97% aa. TC 36.5°C.

Clinical diary:
- Immediate ECG is performed: rapid ventricular response atrial fibrillation (HR 114 bpm), no signs of acute ischaemia, no repolarisation changes. First documented finding.
- Venous access found and troponin, biochemistry, blood count, TSH, pro-BNP, electrolytes performed. Arterial EGA is performed: physiological exchanges, mild hypoxemia (PaO2 72 mmHg).
- Patient is sent to radiology for 2-projection chest X-ray: slight increase in hilar vascular pattern bilaterally, no frank pleural effusion, no parenchymal thickening. Mild enlargement of the cardiac shadow.
- Tests are viewed: troponin <14, negative. No significant indices of inflammation. Blood count normal. TSH normal. Pro-BNP 480 pg/mL (slightly elevated). Potassium 3.8 mEq/L. Sodium 139 mEq/L.
- Bedside echo performed: no pericardial effusion. Modest left atrial dilatation. No diffuse B lines in lung fields. Visually preserved left ventricular systolic function.
- On-call cardiology is contacted for collegial assessment. Given the duration of the episode reported by the patient (approximately 4 hours), the approach is discussed: the patient is haemodynamically stable, therefore a decision is made to check the rate in the first instance.
- Metoprolol tartrate 5mg e.v. is administered under continuous monitoring. ECG check after 20 minutes: persistent AF, HR reduced to 88 bpm. Patient reports slight improvement in symptoms.
- Patient is informed of Diagnosis, need for anticoagulant therapy and related thromboembolic risk. CHA₂DS₂-VASc is calculated: 3 points (according to ESC 2020 guidelines). Indication for oral anticoagulant therapy.
- Patient is admitted to cardiology for in-depth diagnosis, initiation of anticoagulant therapy and evaluation of rhythm vs. rate strategy in election.

Diagnosis: ATRIAL FIBRILLATION OF NEW RAPID VENTRICOLAR RESPONSE

Prognosis: hospitalisation
