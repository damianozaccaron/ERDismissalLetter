What is venous thrombosis? Venous thrombosis is the medical term used when there is a blood clot within a vein. The most common type of venous thrombosis is deep vein thrombosis (DVT) of the leg. Around one in every 1 000 adults each year are affected by venous thrombosis. Most patients suffering from DVT have recently been in hospital (known as hospital acquired thrombosis) or have other risk factors for developing thrombosis. However, in some people, there is no obvious reason for venous thrombosis. In these cases, the venous thrombosis may be described as “unprovoked”, rather than “provoked”, which is used to describe venous thrombosis where there is a clear predisposing cause. When a blood clot forms within a vein, blood can no longer ﬂow in the normal way.
