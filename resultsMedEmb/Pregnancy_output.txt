Clinical prediction rules and D-dimers Diagnosis of PE during pregnancy can be challenging as symptoms frequently overlap with those of normal pregnancy. The overall prevalence of confirmed PE is low among women investigated for the disease, between 2 and 7%.388 D-dimer levels continuously increase during pregnancy, and levels are above the threshold for VTE ‘rule-out’ in almost one-quarter of pregnant women in the third trimester. The results of a multinational prospective management study of 441 pregnant women presenting to emergency departments with clinically suspected PE suggest that a diagnostic strategybased on the assessment of clinical probability, D-dimer measurement, CUS, and CTPAmay safely exclude PE in pregnancy. In that study, PE exclusion on the basis of a negative D-dimer result (without imaging) was possible in 11.% of the 392 women with a non-high pre-test probability (Geneva) score, a rate that was reduced to 4.% in the third trimester.
